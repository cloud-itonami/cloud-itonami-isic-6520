Reinsurance

ISIC Rev.5 6520 · Open Business Blueprint

Treaty and Claims-Recovery Governed for OSS

An open-source business blueprint for risk-transfer contracting between ceding insurers and reinsurers. This actor covers treaty intake through binding and claims-recovery filing through payment, wrapping treaty-LLM proposals in an independent Reinsurance Governor that re-verifies every high-stakes action against spec-cited jurisdiction requirements and this actor's own quota-share/excess-of-loss recovery math.

Binding a treaty and paying a recovery are never autonomous—a human reinsurance underwriter always signs off. Every intake, assessment, binding, filing and payment event is recorded to an immutable audit ledger. Any jurisdiction's bordereaux/collateral requirements, recovery calculation mismatch, or double-payment attempt triggers a mandatory hold, not an override.

This is a forkable business model: you supply the jurisdiction-specific license, actuarial expertise, and payment integrations; the blueprint supplies the governed, spec-cited, audited execution scaffold so you do not have to build the compliance layer from scratch.

Scope & Principles

What it covers: treaty intake with jurisdiction bordereaux/collateral checklisting, treaty binding with independent evidence verification, claims-recovery filing and payment with re-verified recovery calculations, and immutable audit ledger.

What it doesn't cover: licensing (supplied by operator), aggregate deductibles or reinstatement premiums, real transfer-agent or banking-payment integration, retrocession tracking or multi-layer towers.

Built on: langgraph-clj StateGraph runtime (portable .cljc, supervised loop, interrupts, Datomic/in-mem checkpoints) — the same actor pattern as life insurance (6511), non-life insurance (6512), and sister ISIC services.
